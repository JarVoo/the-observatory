# Gibraltar Tourism — Key Findings

## Overview
Analysis of Gibraltar visitor data from 1970 to 2023 across 
three entry points: air, sea, and land frontier.

## Key Findings

### 1. Land crossings dominate
Over 90% of all visitors enter via the land frontier with Spain.
Air and sea arrivals combined represent a small fraction of total 
visitor numbers.

### 2. Peak visitor year was 2011
Total visitors reached 11,940,543 in 2011 — the highest on record.
Numbers have not returned to this level despite a partial recovery 
post-COVID.

### 3. Frontier reopening transformed tourism
When Spain reopened the frontier in 1982 after a 13-year closure,
land crossings grew from zero to over 2 million within three years.
By 1990 land arrivals exceeded 4 million annually.

### 4. COVID impact was severe
2019 total: 11,336,422 visitors
2020 total: 5,437,728 visitors
Decline: 52% year on year

### 5. Recovery is incomplete
2023 figures of 9,282,911 remain 18% below pre-COVID 2019 levels.
Air and sea recovery has been stronger than land frontier recovery.

## Data Source
Gibraltar Statistics Office
Period: 1970 - 2023
